Centralia Car OS

A minimalistic, intuitive, and responsive Car OS designed for future car users.

DURATION

3-Months course project

MY ROLE

UX Designer

TEAM MEMBER

TOOLS

Figma, Adobe Illustrator, Notability

SKILLS

Sketching, Moodboard, Personas, Storyboarding, Wireframes, Prototyping, Usability Testing, Presentation

The Context

Centralia Car OS is designed as my class project for Mobile Application Design (INFO 365). This class challenges us as designers to create an unique UX operating system that could be imagined to be on any physical devices. In the 3-month duration, we are asked to go through 15 potential application for our operating system based on our design language and design principle. Our team was the only team in the class that decided to challenge ourselves by creating an unique car user experience. Hope you enjoy reading our journey.

What is Centralia Car OS?

Centralia Car OS is a futuristic example, which provides a wholistic driving experience through apps designed for every step of the journey, creating interactions both in and out of the car.

It utilizes the car’s windshield, a console screen, and personal smartphones to create a seamless, multimodal driving experience. Through thoughtful designs and trackings across physical devices, Centralia uses ride-related personal data to provide a better user experience for both drivers and passengers.

Design Language

Essential

Being minimal and sleek, powerful yet not distracting, “Essential” creates a unique experience for drivers that incorporates intuitive & responsive designs to promote safety. It becomes one with the road and one with you.

The Process

Research & Literature Analysis

In order to develop a Car OS that is comfortable for our users, our team decides to look for what has already been built on the market. Through our research and literature analysis, we pull out the following elements that drivers found helpful or distracting from the existing car practices. To communicate emotions, we added colored sticky notes: Blue - Informative, Green - Foundamental, Yellow - Unsure, Orange - Try to Avoid.

Ideation

Based on our research and team brainstorm, we wrote out key words on the whiteboard and draw connections. Through this process, we were able to identify common themes and use these to drive our design decisions.

User Journey

Based on what we have know, our team designed a driver's user journey, which divides the user experience into three stages: before trip, while driving, and post trip.

  • Before Trip

    Drivers can prepare for a trip through their smartphone and continue to make adjustments in the car through the console screen.

  • While Driving

    Drivers could interact with Centralia OS through either the console screen or vocal commands.

  • Post Trip

    Drivers may use functionalities on their smartphone to improve their future driving experience.

Usability Testing

We found out that by projecting car statistics onto the windshield, it ensures the drivers safety by decreasing the frequency of the drivers lowering their head. To figure out the exact position, we read through several eye tracking research and conducted several rounds of usability testing with our team members using paper prototype. In the end, we agree on putting it on the lower center of the screen as it appears to be the most non-intrusive way for the drivers.

Sketches, Low-fidelity Prototype, User Flow

Every single one of our app are design through lots of iterations, especially at the beginning when we have not contructed our UI component library. In fact, we went through a lot of changes - from windshield to console screen, from gestures to tapping actions. Due to the fast-paced of the course, we also had to make smart choices on our design choices, figuring out important parts of the design that convey most.

UX Patterns & UI Components

High Fidelity Mock-Up Apps

Main Page & Find My Car

Users can easily locate their car through the car’s companion app.

Car Adjustment

Users can set their preferences prior to getting into their car through their smartphone, allowing the car to adapt to the user automatically. The preferences can be manually changed in the car through the console as well.

Home/Location Response

Users could save their targeted location and correspond connected devices on the companion app, and then preset each device according to distance or time to arrive. According to the car’s distance or the amount of time to the destination, devices will be activated accordingly. Drivers will be notified when the devices is being activated.

Music

Users are able to set their default playlists before entering the car.

Users are able to update their music while driving through the console screen and voice commands.

Map

User could save their favorites places or access their recent search history on the companion app. Users can set their destination through the companion app.

The console provides a trip overview as well as a map view that supplements the directions on the windshield. Users receives traffic notifications such as school zones, tolls, construction sites and more. The color of the current speed also indicates when drivers are driving at unsafe speeds.

Trip Analysis

Users are able to gain insight from data collections and into their driving habits to improve future driving experiences.

Presentation / Final Deliverable

Our team presented every two weeks to working professionals who are familiar with mobile application design to gain their feedbacks on our design. During each of our presentation, we talked though reasonings behind our design, including our design process and collaborations. Our final prototype received rating - "4.7 out of 5 stars", the 2nd place among seven groups.
