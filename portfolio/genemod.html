The Problem

Driving has always been a problem for most: tiredness after a long day of work, high cost of fuel and parking, heavy traffiic along side with the pollution ... It tightly relate to our day-to-day life and our future environment.

Carpool, in our opinion, is a great way of solving these problem. By creating a way for people to carpool to work, we can reduce the number of cars on the road, and subsequently the carbon emissions we have.

What is CarpoolRide?

CarpoolRide is an app that designed to help users find reliable people to carpool with. It focuses on finding passengers and drivers that match a person’s preferences. It also allows for clear communication of rating systems for drivers and passengers, whether it be by ratings, reviews, or other methods.

User Research

Questionnaire-based Survey

We utilized a questionnaire-based survey to explore different factors that people may consider important when using transportation methods. At the same time, we want to use these quantitatively data to learn about the common reoccuring practices people have when carpooling

Semi-Structured Interviews

We conducted 5 interview to breakdown different aspects of carpooling, such as the importance of community and why people choose to carpool or not. Having a semi-structured approach allows us to tailor the interview to focus on the aspects the participant considers important (safety, finance, etc).

Interview Protocol

Interview Highlights

Insights - 4 Major Themes

Safety

Safety is especially relevant when carpooling with people you aren’t that familiar with, such as friends of friends or complete strangers.

Personal Preferences

Personal preference is a underlying factor in the decision of ending a carpool. In particular, understanding the driver’s driving habits, riders' preferred car’s temperatures, car's cleanliness, and even music tastes all contribute to carpoolers’ happiness and comforts.

Time Schedule

The concept of matching schedules and flexible times was a major recurring theme in both the semi-structured interviews and survey results. Participants do not feel comfortable about modifying their own or the other carpoolers’ schedules.

Transportation Convenience

Convenience was repeatedly mentioned as a key factor in the decision to carpool. The convenience of carpooling could depend on the cost, people they ride with, and taken time.

Task Analysis

We designed the following six tasks based on the major themes. These tasks are iterated and revised throughout our design proess. Our team uses these tasks to inform our design choices.

  • Task 1

    Finding carpoolers on same routes

  • Task 2

    Communicate current locations and status

  • Task 3

    Finding the most efficient routes

  • Task 4

    Finding a community with shared preferences

  • Task 5

    Monitoring Driving Behaviors to Ensure Safety of Carpoolers

  • Task 6

    See money saved/C02 emissions from carpooling

3 Proposed Design

We proposed 3 different designs based on the six tasks, which each of the design was informed by different set of tasks.

  • Design 1

    Finding preference matched people & Encourage safe carpool practice

  • Design 2

    Gamification design to encourage continuous carpool practice

  • Design 3

    In-car softwares to help facilitate carpool experience

Decision

We chose to pursue our first design centered around finding and matching with individuals, based on their preferences. It enforces the concept of "safety" and "comfort" by allowing clear communication between drivers and passengers, writing ratings and reviews, etc. Many participants also cited things such as heavy traffic and missing bus schedules as pain points of their current transportation method. From this, we would like this design to communicate a reliable yet flexible schedule.

Storyboarding

Task 1 - Finding suitable carpoolers

Our user research overwhelmingly showed that people wanted to know their carpooling group first before the carpooling experience. They focused a lot on having a network of reliable passengers through filtering with background checks for security and not random strangers. When asked the most important factor when deciding to carpool with someone, almost every participant mentioned “comfortability with other passengers”, “relationship”, and “reliability”. This is why we chose to center around the design based on finding suitable carpoolers.

Task 2 - Communicating live updates

Most of our research participants mentioned that delays and unexpected scheduling changes were the main problems with their carpooling experiences. We wanted to make sure this is included in our design as our targeted participants of students and working professionals mentioned that schedule changes and road-related delays were common.

Onboarding

Users will first set up their accounts and then be asked to fill out their preferences.

As a Driver

Pre-filled preferences

If users plan to use the app as a driver, they will be asked to scan their driver licenses and fill out their car’s information. Then, they will be asked for their preferences both as a driver and as a rider.

Create a ride as a driver

If users plan to be the driver of a ride, they can create a ride by filling in the ride information.

Add designated carpoolers

They could either invite their friends or choose potential carpoolers from the suggested list created by the app.

These suggested carpoolers are chosen based on the given ride route, time schedule, and their preferences. Each carpooler profile contains information about how much they are willing to pay for the ride and the additional time this will add to the entire ride.

Communicate and plan ahead

Added passengers will need to first confirm that they want to be added to the ride first before drivers can start the ride.

Once passengers confirm, drivers will be able to see the trip’s detail and communicate with their riders.

Starting the trip with one step

The drivers will receive a notification when the ride is about to start soon. When the driver taps to ‘Start’ to start the ride, all corresponding passengers will receive a notification while the driver will see the navigation screen heading towards the first passenger to be picked up.

When the drivers are close to the destination, they could send remind messages to rider by tapping the auto-generated message button

As a Rider

Pre-filled preferences

If users plan to use the app solely as a rider, they will only be asked to fill out their preference as a rider. All of these preferences could be adjusted later in the settings.

Request a ride as a rider

If users plan to join a ride as a rider, they may send out a request by filling in the ride information.

Secure and trustworthy selections

After receiving approval from drivers, they will be able to compare and select the ones’ that best fit with their needs.

Communicate and plan ahead

After joining the carpool, they will be able to see the trip’s detail and communicate with the driver and other riders before and during the trip.
